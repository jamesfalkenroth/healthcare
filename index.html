FQH-Seeing Health Access Among Bruins

Sharing stories of awareness and usage of Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) among UCLA students.

Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) are federally-funded nonprofit health centers and clinics which serve low-income and underserved populations.

We are a group of UCLA undergraduate students interested in exploring the effects of FQHCs in the UCLA community. Through our survey, we aim to measure the awareness and use of FQHCs among undergraduate students in the UCLA community in order to visualize potential areas of change for the community to better utilize FQHC resources.
